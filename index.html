Tracking Footfall at the Kibble Palace

"Designing with care for heritage, people, and plants."

Project Background

The Kibble Palace is a magnificent 19th-century glasshouse covering 2,137m² in Glasgow Botanic Gardens – publicly-owned gardens managed by Glasgow City Council. This architectural marvel, constructed from curved wrought iron and glass supported by cast-iron beams, initially served as an exhibition venue before transitioning to house plants in the 1880s. Today, it contains a remarkable collection of flora including the national collection of Australasian tree ferns.

As one of Glasgow's most popular tourist attractions, the Kibble Palace welcomes visitors from around the world while serving as a beloved space for local residents. However, Glasgow City Council faces a significant challenge: effectively measuring visitor footfall to make informed decisions about resource allocation, staffing, and long-term planning.

This website documents our journey in developing a heritage-sensitive, privacy-respecting footfall tracking solution that balances technological innovation with the preservation requirements of a Category A Listed building. We hope that exploring our process will provide insights into designing IoT solutions for heritage environments and inspire similar approaches for other historic venues facing comparable challenges.

The Challenge

Glasgow City Council struggles to accurately measure visitor numbers at the Kibble Palace, limiting their ability to:

  • Optimize staffing levels based on peak visiting times
  • Understand seasonal and weather-related visitor patterns
  • Justify funding requests with concrete usage data
  • Plan maintenance schedules around visitor flow
  • Assess the impact of special events and exhibitions

The building's status as a Category A Listed structure presents unique constraints. Any technological solution must avoid structural modifications while being safe for the diverse plant life and resident koi fish. Additionally, the solution must respect visitor privacy and remain completely unobtrusive to maintain the serene, heritage atmosphere that visitors expect.

Impact & Benefits

Our solution enables Glasgow City Council to:

  • Make data-driven staffing decisions, optimizing resource allocation
  • Understand visitor patterns to improve the overall experience
  • Demonstrate the Kibble Palace's popularity to secure future funding
  • Plan maintenance and conservation work around visitor flows
  • Measure the success of events and marketing initiatives
  • Provide evidence for tourism impact assessments

Beyond immediate operational benefits, this project demonstrates how heritage sites can embrace modern technology responsibly. Our approach respects both the physical constraints of historic buildings and the ethical considerations of visitor privacy, creating a model that other heritage venues can adapt and implement.

This website reflects our journey in developing a footfall tracking solution for the Kibble Palace, showcasing our design process, technical decisions, and iterative refinements. We hope our approach inspires other heritage sites to explore how technology can enhance operations while preserving what makes these spaces special.
